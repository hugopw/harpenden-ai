# Brief - Harpenden.AI

Source: dictated by Hugo Pickford-Wardle, 2026-04-22. Tidied but not re-interpreted.

## The question

What happens when a whole town - not an organisation - becomes AI-fluent and uses that fluency collectively to solve community problems and then carries that back into their workplaces?

## Why Harpenden, why now

- Harpenden has a high concentration of London-based knowledge workers in large corporates where the current corporate business case is to reduce headcount.
- These residents are expensive, senior, and therefore disproportionately exposed to AI-driven displacement.
- They have also invested heavily in their children's education on the assumption university is the route to a career like theirs - an assumption that is now shakier.
- The town therefore has both a real downside risk and an unusual concentration of capable people who could engage with AI fluency at pace.

## The outcomes Hugo wants

1. **Minimise job-displacement risk** - Hugo's personal mission, which this project directly advances.
2. **Solve systemic community problems** using AI - traffic, loneliness, event venues, potholes, council engagement.
3. **Prove the model** - a vibrant AI hub at town-community scale, reducing town-council overhead while increasing its impact.
4. **Demonstrate meaningful human connection** at community (not organisational) level.
5. **Commercial** - this is chargeable. AI Night School, Sherpas AI, AI Optimist offerings get high-volume exposure in one location, building brand awareness and revenue.
6. **Expansion** - prove it in Harpenden, then port the pattern to other well-chosen towns.

## The mechanics already in Hugo's hands

- Segmentation of community groups and their learning journeys already done.
- Programmes already designed per group.
- AI Night School and Sherpas AI content ready to push through at volume.
- The Harpenden Collective + AI Night School alumni form a starter directory.
- A multi-generational angle already works: teenagers (Sherpas AI work experience), working-age (AI Night School), retirees, council staff.

## What the website needs to do

The site IS the pitch to the town council. When Hugo walks them through it, they should see:

- The vision fully formed.
- The risk clearly articulated.
- The subgroups identified (residents by demographic, businesses by type).
- The journey to get there laid out - specifically, what the council can contribute (flyers, letterbox drops, connections into Rothamsted, the Leisure Centre, noticeboards, social channels, Harpenden Facebook group).
- A directory of interventions the town can draw on.
- A worked example prototype (`/harpenden-traffic`).
- A council-facing version of the AI Transformation Accelerator - how the council can use AI to run itself better and increase community engagement.
- Offers: AI Night School and AI Optimist (with possible Harpenden-resident special value - design TBD), the "become an AI Coach" train-the-trainer offer, community groups organised around local priorities, and physical spaces for AI work with support.
- A vibrant summer festival concept: Harpenden AI (festival). Acknowledge summer-holiday constraint and that this needs to run through community members, not solely Hugo.

Conversion goal for the council meeting: the council leaves the meeting ready to help, not debating whether to engage.
